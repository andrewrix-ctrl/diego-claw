AI in Australia Daily Brief — 26 May 2026

Australia-focused AI developments with practical, story-specific implications for Fujitsu.

Last updated: 25 May 2026 (AEST)

Diego's take

Today's signal: AI adoption is getting operational, governed and local.

Today's Australia-relevant AI news is less about glossy demos and more about enterprise muscle memory: Rio Tinto is using AI to recover knowledge from legacy industrial systems, Lendi is baking AI behaviour into performance management, blueAPACHE is turning agentic AI into a managed mid-market service, and DXC and ServiceNow are pushing governance as the control tower for production agents. The policy layer is moving too, with Australia and the UK formalising AI safety cooperation and renewed calls for a cabinet-level sovereign capability plan.

Broad Fujitsu implication: Fujitsu should lean into AI as operating-model change, not tool rollout. The opportunity is to help clients modernise legacy knowledge, govern agentic workflows, build data and cyber foundations, support workforce adoption, and connect sovereign AI ambition to practical delivery patterns.

MiningLegacy modernisationData foundationsSoftware delivery

Rio Tinto uses AI to document a 30-year-old manufacturing system

Summary: iTnews reports Rio Tinto is using AI to document a decades-old manufacturing system across its Australia and New Zealand aluminium operations, turning hard-to-access legacy knowledge into usable technical documentation.

Implication for Fujitsu: This is a strong pattern for industrial clients with ageing core systems. Fujitsu can package legacy discovery, code/documentation analysis, knowledge capture and migration planning as a lower-risk path into modernisation before clients attempt major platform replacement.

Source: iTnews

Lendi adds AI use to annual performance reviews

Summary: iTnews reports Lendi Group is factoring employee AI use into annual performance reviews as part of its commitment to become AI-native, moving AI adoption from optional experimentation into expected work practice.

Implication for Fujitsu: Clients will increasingly ask how to measure AI adoption without damaging trust. Fujitsu should pair productivity programs with role-specific guidance, acceptable-use controls, evidence-based metrics, manager training and safeguards for employee data and fairness.

Source: iTnews

DXC and ServiceNow pitch AI governance as the agentic control tower

Summary: iTnews partner content says DXC and ServiceNow are positioning governance as the defining challenge for enterprise AI agents as they move from test cases into real-world decisions and workflows.

Implication for Fujitsu: The message is directly relevant to Fujitsu's transformation and managed-services work: production agents need policy enforcement, workflow ownership, human escalation, logging, service management integration and clear accountability before they touch critical client processes.

Source: iTnews

blueAPACHE turns agentic AI adoption into a mid-market managed service

Summary: ARN reports Australian MSP blueAPACHE says a Microsoft E7 customer win shows mid-market and SMB appetite for agentic AI, while also exposing the need for guardrails, visibility, ISO-aligned governance, training and observability.

Implication for Fujitsu: Mid-market clients will need packaged help, not bespoke strategy decks. Fujitsu can compete by productising readiness assessments, Microsoft ecosystem controls, adoption training, monitoring and governance-as-a-service for smaller clients that lack internal AI risk teams.

Source: ARN

Australia and the UK sign an AI safety pact

Summary: InnovationAus reports Australia and the UK have signed an AI safety memorandum of understanding, with the planned Australian AI Safety Institute set to work with its UK counterpart on frontier-model monitoring, testing practices, joint research and cyber-risk concerns.

Implication for Fujitsu: Public-sector and regulated clients will expect AI assurance to align with emerging international practice. Fujitsu should prepare reusable model-risk, testing, red-teaming and reporting approaches that can support sovereign AI programs without slowing delivery to a crawl.

Source: InnovationAus
Sovereign capabilityPolicyProcurementNational resilience

Ed Husic calls for a cabinet-level sovereign capability plan

Summary: InnovationAus reports former Industry minister Ed Husic wants sovereign capability coordinated through cabinet, arguing every portfolio should show how it contributes to national resilience rather than leaving capability development haphazard.

Implication for Fujitsu: Sovereign AI will sit inside a broader resilience agenda. Fujitsu should connect AI, cloud, cyber, data-centre, procurement and local skills proposals to measurable sovereign capability outcomes, especially for government and critical infrastructure clients.

Source: InnovationAus

V2 AI scales leadership as demand for enterprise AI delivery grows

Summary: CRN Australia reports AI consultancy V2 AI has hired a managing director, partner and director after near-100 percent revenue growth in 2025, with plans to expand architecture, agentic AI, data platform capability and repeatable IP.

Implication for Fujitsu: Specialist consultancies are scaling around the same gap Fujitsu needs to own: moving clients from ambition to value. Fujitsu should sharpen reusable accelerators, data-quality offers, AI literacy programs and industry-specific delivery playbooks so scale does not dilute delivery quality.

Source: CRN Australia
