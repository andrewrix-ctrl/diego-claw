AI in Australia Daily Brief — 25 May 2026

Australia-focused AI developments with practical, story-specific implications for Fujitsu.

Last updated: 25 May 2026 (AEST)

Diego's take

Today’s signal: AI is moving from announcements into operating models, channel motions and production controls.

Today’s Australia-relevant AI news has a strong enterprise delivery flavour. CBA is using agents inside incident response and workforce feedback loops, EML is folding AI into managed security operations, Tasmania is funding an AI sandbox while cutting public-sector costs, and the channel is being reshaped by Microsoft, Cisco, ServiceNow, Cursor and Crayon around local capacity, skills, secure AI infrastructure and outcome-led services.

Broad Fujitsu implication: Fujitsu should treat this as a market signal for practical AI transformation: clients need secure production patterns, observability, data quality, cloud and data-centre strategy, workforce governance, measurable benefits and managed services that reduce delivery risk as AI moves into core work.

Financial servicesAgentic AISoftware deliveryOperations

CBA puts a DevOps agent into after-hours incident response

Summary: iTnews reports Commonwealth Bank is testing an AWS frontier AI DevOps agent that begins incident investigation when PagerDuty alerts fire, giving on-call engineers a likely root cause and remediation options by the time they log in.

Implication for Fujitsu: This is a concrete production pattern for agentic AI in critical operations. Fujitsu can help financial-services and infrastructure clients design guarded incident-response agents with audit trails, human approval points, secure telemetry access and measurable reductions in mean time to diagnose.

Source: iTnews
CyberInsuranceManaged servicesData protection

Employers Mutual Limited shifts security operations toward AI-backed XDR

Summary: iTnews reports Australian insurance services provider EML is using XDR, an AI-powered cloud SOC and cloud SIEM services from SentinelOne to improve visibility, automate response and protect sensitive personal and medical data.

Implication for Fujitsu: Regulated clients with high-value personal data will want security programs that combine tooling, tuning and managed operations. Fujitsu should emphasise SIEM modernisation, alert-quality improvement, data protection controls and supplier governance rather than selling AI security as a standalone feature.

Source: iTnews
Financial servicesWorkforceAutomationAI governance

CBA uses AI to analyse staff feedback and suggest follow-up actions

Summary: iTnews reports CBA is applying AI to employee surveys across more than 51,000 staff, analysing large volumes of free-text comments, asking adaptive follow-up questions and helping leaders identify actions while keeping human judgement in the loop.

Implication for Fujitsu: Workforce AI projects need transparent review loops because they affect trust, culture and employment decisions. Fujitsu can help clients define guardrails for sensitive people data, bias checks, action tracking and manager accountability before scaling similar productivity tools.

Source: iTnews
GovernmentPublic sectorAI governanceDigital uplift

Tasmania funds an AI sandbox inside a wider digital uplift

Summary: InnovationAus reports Tasmania’s budget includes an ongoing $61 million digital uplift and a $4 million artificial intelligence sandbox, positioned against broader public-sector spending cuts and planned job reductions.

Implication for Fujitsu: Public-sector AI demand may be tied directly to productivity pressure. Fujitsu should frame AI sandbox work around safe experimentation, benefits tracking, workflow redesign and workforce impact management so agencies do not confuse budget relief with uncontrolled automation.

Source: InnovationAus
Sovereign AIInfrastructureCyberSkills

Microsoft announces a $25 billion Australia AI, cloud and cyber push

Summary: CRN Australia reports Microsoft used its Sydney AI Tour to announce a $25 billion Australian investment covering AI and cloud infrastructure, cyber defence and a plan to train at least three million Australians with workforce-ready AI skills by 2028.

Implication for Fujitsu: More local capacity will raise client expectations rather than solve adoption by itself. Fujitsu should pair hyperscale options with sovereign architecture choices, cost governance, cyber uplift, skilling pathways and industry-specific use cases that turn infrastructure into measurable outcomes.

Source: CRN Australia
AI factoryInfrastructureSovereign AIData centres

Cisco, Sharon AI and NVIDIA launch a secure AI factory in Australia

Summary: CRN Australia reports Cisco has partnered with Sharon AI and NVIDIA on its first secure AI factory in Australia, hosted by NextDC, with Blackwell Ultra GPUs, sandbox access and a first Sydney pod planned before expansion across A/NZ.

Implication for Fujitsu: Local AI factory capacity gives clients another route for sensitive workloads and proof-of-concepts. Fujitsu should assess where this model fits against public cloud, private cloud and edge options, especially for data residency, networking, cyber controls and sustainability constraints.

Source: CRN Australia
Agentic AIChannelWorkflowSoftware delivery

ServiceNow reworks its Build partner program for AI agents

Summary: CRN Australia reports ServiceNow has refreshed its Build Partner Program so ISVs, developers and technology partners can build, test, certify and distribute AI Platform solutions, with ANZ leadership pointing to demand for industry-specific workflow AI.

Implication for Fujitsu: Workflow platforms are becoming a commercial route for AI agents. Fujitsu should build reusable industry accelerators with clear certification, support and governance models rather than relying on bespoke AI pilots that are hard to scale or monetise.

Source: CRN Australia

Cursor hires an Australia and New Zealand regional head

Summary: ARN reports AI coding start-up Cursor has appointed Pete Short as regional vice president for A/NZ, based in Sydney, as it builds its go-to-market presence with partners and customers across the region.

Implication for Fujitsu: AI coding assistants are becoming a local enterprise sales and partner contest. Fujitsu should keep developer productivity strategy vendor-neutral, with security review, licence controls, SDLC integration and metrics for quality, maintainability and delivery speed.

Source: ARN
ChannelManaged servicesAI adoptionCommercial models

Crayon says AI is pushing MSPs from transactions to outcome-led services

Summary: ARN reports Crayon’s A/NZ channel leadership sees distributors and MSPs facing parallel change as licence margins shrink and AI complexity pushes partners toward specialisation, services funds, gain-share models and customer transformation work.

Implication for Fujitsu: The channel economics around AI are shifting toward risk-sharing and measurable outcomes. Fujitsu should expect more pressure for value-based delivery models, partner specialisation and managed services that prove savings or productivity gains rather than simply reselling platforms.

Source: ARN
