Recent AI developments in Australia + what it means for Fujitsu

This page is structured for daily refreshes, each item includes a practical implication for Fujitsu in Australia.

Last updated: Sunday, April 19th, 2026

Anthropic-Australia Partnership: First Under National AI Plan

Australia has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Anthropic, marking the first formal agreement under Australia's National AI Plan. The partnership includes collaboration with Australia's AI Safety Institute, AUD $3 million in Claude API credits for four research institutions, and exploration of data centre infrastructure in Australia.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu should monitor this partnership for opportunities in AI safety research, potential collaboration with the research institutions receiving Claude API credits, and be aware of Anthropic's growing operational commitment in the Australian market.

GPT-5.4 Scores 75.0% on OSWorld, Exceeding Human Expert Baseline

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 has achieved a 75.0% score on the OSWorld benchmark for desktop autonomy, surpassing the human expert baseline of 72.4%. This marks the first time an AI model has exceeded human performance in navigating desktop environments, enabling it to perform multi-step workflows across various applications.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu should evaluate how advanced AI models like GPT-5.4 can be integrated into its internal operations and client solutions to automate complex desktop tasks, thereby enhancing efficiency and exploring potential new service offerings in agentic automation.

Shift from Co-pilot to Autonomous: The Rise of Agentic AI

The article highlights the fundamental difference between traditional 'co-pilot' AI, which assists humans, and 'agentic AI,' which operates autonomously to plan and execute multi-step workflows towards a given goal. This shift changes the human role from operator to supervisor at critical decision points.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu should strategically assess migrating from co-pilot AI implementations to agentic AI where appropriate, particularly in areas requiring end-to-end process automation. This demands a renewed focus on defining clear guardrails and human oversight mechanisms.

Australia Leads on Responsible AI Governance, But Lags in Productivity Gains from AI

KPMG's Global AI Pulse survey reveals that Australian businesses lead in adopting AI governance policies (31% vs 26% globally) to ensure trustworthy AI. However, only 35% prioritise AI-driven productivity, lower than the global average of 42%, indicating a gap in leveraging AI for efficiency.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu can leverage Australia’s strong focus on responsible AI as a competitive advantage by emphasizing ethical AI in its offerings. Simultaneously, it should help clients, and itself, bridge the productivity gap by demonstrating tangible AI-driven efficiency improvements in its solutions.

Upcoming Key Regulatory Dates for AI in Australia (2026)

Australia's principles-based National AI Plan (Dec 2025) avoids prescriptive AI legislation like the EU AI Act. Key upcoming dates in 2026 include Government AI Policy v2.0 for APS agencies (June 2026) and Privacy Act ADM Tranche 1 (December 2026) introducing transparency obligations for automated decisions.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu must ensure its AI solutions and internal practices comply with the Government AI Policy v2.0 for engagements with APS agencies by June 2026. Furthermore, preparations for the Privacy Act ADM Tranche 1 by December 2026 are necessary to ensure transparency and explainability in automated decision-making processes.

Adobe Unveils Firefly AI Assistant to Streamline Creative Workflows

Adobe has introduced Firefly AI Assistant, a conversational tool for creative work across its software portfolio, entering public beta soon. It enables users to describe desired outcomes in natural language, orchestrating multi-step processes across applications like Photoshop and Premiere, embodying the shift towards agentic AI systems.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu should observe the adoption and impact of agentic AI tools like Adobe Firefly, especially in creative and design-intensive client sectors. This can inform the development of similar workflow automation solutions or strategic partnerships to enhance Fujitsu's offerings in digital transformation, focusing on seamless multi-application integration.

Canva Launches AI 2.0: AI Architecture Layer for Editable Designs

Canva has unveiled AI 2.0, its most significant product update since 2013, integrating an AI architecture layer across its platform. This allows users to create and edit designs using natural language and voice, orchestrating multiple tools and workflows to streamline creative processes and expand into various office tasks.

Source

Implication for Fujitsu: Fujitsu should analyze Canva's strategy of embedding AI directly into creative workflows, integrating multiple tools, and expanding into routine office tasks. This approach has implications for developing user-friendly AI solutions that cater to a broad user base and for enhancing collaboration across various software ecosystems.
